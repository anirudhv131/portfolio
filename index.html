Behavioral Finance

Does Retail Attention Predict Returns? Evidence from Robinhood "Popularity" Data, 2018 to 2020

Using historical Robinhood "popularity" data alongside daily prices for 57 stocks, this paper asks whether a sudden spike in retail attention predicts how a stock performs afterward. Measured against a stock's own history, popularity spikes carry no signal at all. But measured relative to the rest of the market, a proxy for landing on a "Top Movers" list, stocks that stand out underperform the rest of the sample by about 1 percentage point over the following week, a statistically significant result.

Sports Economics

What Predicts World Cup Success?

The standard "Soccernomics" view is that a country's population and wealth largely determine how well its national team performs. Testing this across 63 countries that have played regularly in the World Cup since 1994, population and GDP per capita together explain very little of the variation in results. A single variable, a country's accumulated World Cup experience, explains nearly four times as much on its own, and is the only significant predictor once all three are considered together.

Housing Policy

Reforming Credit Access: Addressing Structural Exclusion in Urban Homeownership

For millions of working-class urban residents, barriers to mortgage credit block the path to homeownership and wealth-building. This paper examines two structural failures — a credit scoring system that ignores rent payment history, and a regulatory framework that exempts nonbank lenders from community investment obligations — and proposes targeted reforms to address each.
