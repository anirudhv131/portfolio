Equity Research · Pair Trade · Jun 20, 2026

Long Texas Roadhouse, Short Cava

This is a market-neutral paired position that holds Texas Roadhouse long and Cava short in equal dollar amounts, so it makes money only if Texas Roadhouse outperforms Cava regardless of where the sector goes. The case rests on a split in the 2026 consumer. Texas Roadhouse posted 7.1% comparable sales growth on 4.5% traffic growth in Q1, which is the better kind of comp because it comes from more guests rather than price alone, and it still trades about 10% below its 52-week high. Cava, meanwhile, has run roughly 54% in six months and now trades well above what the business currently earns, at the exact moment the premium end of the restaurant market faces the most consumer pressure. Both companies report Q2 results in August, with Cava due on August 11, which is the main near-term catalyst for the spread.

Equity Research · Jun 15, 2026

The Cigna Group (NYSE: CI): A Healthcare Giant Mid-Transition

Cigna has become the subject of unusually wide disagreement on the Street, with one bank raising its price target to $400 and another cutting to $302 off the same set of facts. This report builds a discounted cash flow model from Cigna's segment-level financials and historical cash conversion, arriving at a $319 price target, roughly 12% above where the stock traded at the time of writing. The thesis is that the market is treating a pharmacy-benefit pricing shift and two business divestitures as pure downside when the underlying cash generation tells a different story.

Cross-market correlation

What moves together

Pearson correlation of hourly changes in implied probability over the past seven days, across a basket of the most liquid mid-range Polymarket markets. Differencing strips out shared drift, so the matrix reflects genuine co-movement rather than a common trend. Correlation is not causation.

Leading indicators

What tends to move first

For every pair in the basket, the cross-correlation is measured at lags of one to six hours. A market is credited with leading another when its earlier moves line up with the other's later moves more strongly than the reverse, above a 0.15 threshold. Seven days of hourly data is a small sample, so read this as an exploratory signal rather than settled structure.

Large traders

Who is behind the size

Ranked from Polymarket's on-chain trader leaderboard by realized profit, the platform's own settled figure, with traded volume alongside as a measure of track record. If the leaderboard is unavailable, this falls back to the largest wallets in the recent public trade feed, with their net direction. Names link to public Polymarket profiles.

Category predictability

Which subjects the crowd prices well

Brier 48h is the mean squared gap between the implied probability roughly two days before a market resolved and what actually happened, measured over recent decisively resolved Polymarket markets. Lower means better calibrated, so lower means more predictable. Decisive is how far the category's live markets sit from a coin flip on average. Categories with fewer than three resolved samples show a dash.
