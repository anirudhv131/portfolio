Equity Research · Pair Trade · Jun 20, 2026

Long Texas Roadhouse, Short Cava

This is a market-neutral paired position that holds Texas Roadhouse long and Cava short in equal dollar amounts, so it makes money only if Texas Roadhouse outperforms Cava regardless of where the sector goes. The case rests on a split in the 2026 consumer. Texas Roadhouse posted 7.1% comparable sales growth on 4.5% traffic growth in Q1, which is the better kind of comp because it comes from more guests rather than price alone, and it still trades about 10% below its 52-week high. Cava, meanwhile, has run roughly 54% in six months and now trades well above what the business currently earns, at the exact moment the premium end of the restaurant market faces the most consumer pressure. Both companies report Q2 results in August, with Cava due on August 11, which is the main near-term catalyst for the spread.

Equity Research · Jun 15, 2026

The Cigna Group (NYSE: CI): A Healthcare Giant Mid-Transition

Cigna has become the subject of unusually wide disagreement on the Street, with one bank raising its price target to $400 and another cutting to $302 off the same set of facts. This report builds a discounted cash flow model from Cigna's segment-level financials and historical cash conversion, arriving at a $319 price target, roughly 12% above where the stock traded at the time of writing. The thesis is that the market is treating a pharmacy-benefit pricing shift and two business divestitures as pure downside when the underlying cash generation tells a different story.
